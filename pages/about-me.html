About me


Hi, My name is Mariska van Willigen. 26 years old and I work as a Machine Learning Engineer for Albert Heijn. In this blog, I'll be your guide as we navigate the intricate terrain of machine learning, one insightful post at a time. At Albert Heijn, I'm currently immersed in the exciting realm of the Dynamic Markdown project, where data-driven decisions fuel help to decrease foodwaste.

My academic path, though not a typical one for a machine learning engineer, has been a fascinating journey. With a Bachelor's degree in Psychology from Leiden University, I bring a unique perspective to the field—one that values the human element behind the data. I continued my quest for knowledge with a Master's degree in Statistical Science for the Life and Behavioral Sciences, specializing in the Data Science track. The blend of psychology and statistics has given me a profound appreciation for the power of data in understanding human behavior.

Beyond the confines of algorithms, I'm a connoisseur of culinary adventures. I live for fancy dinners and can spot a good data point from a mile away. Plus, when I'm not busy unraveling the mysteries of AI, you can find me on the korfball field, proudly representing HKV Achilles—our secret weapon? Data-driven winning strategies, of course!

So, whether you're a fellow data enthusiast, a curious learner, or simply someone looking to demystify the wonders of machine learning, I invite you to join me on this insightful journey.

See you soon!
